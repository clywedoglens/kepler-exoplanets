Features and Models

During our data exploration and engineering, we made a heatmap looking at what values correlate the most with others. Here it is again.

We focus specifically on what columns correlate the most with koi_pdisposition. In this case, we decide to pick the features of "koi_teq", "koi_model_snr", "koi_duration", "koi_impact", and "koi_depth" to train our models. We include a table of the features and their definition from the Kepler website

Feature Concept Definition
koi_teq Equilibrium Temperature (Kelvin) Approximation for the temperature of the planet.
koi_model_snr Transit Signal-to-Noise Transit depth normalized by the mean uncertainty in the flux during the transits.
koi_duration Transit Duration (hours) The duration of the observed transits. Duration is measured from first contact between the planet and star until last contact.
koi_impact Impact Parameter The sky-projected distance between the center of the stellar disc and the center of the planet disc at conjunction, normalized by the stellar radius.
koi_depth Transit Depth (parts per million) The fraction of stellar flux lost at the minimum of the planetary transit.
